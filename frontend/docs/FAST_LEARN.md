## 1. Purpose

Fast Learn Mode is designed for situations where the student needs **quick clarity and recall**, not deep understanding.  
Its goal is to surface the **core ideas of a concept in minimum time**.

---

## 2. What This Mode Does

Fast Learn Mode:

- Extracts and presents **key takeaways only** from the question.

- Uses **short, direct bullet points** for rapid reading.

- Focuses on **remembering the core idea**, not full conceptual depth.

- Avoids long explanations, derivations, or exam-style structuring.

- Helps with **last-minute revision and quick refresh**.

---

## 3. What This Mode Avoids

Fast Learn Mode deliberately avoids:

- Detailed or structured explanations.

- Step-by-step teaching or deep reasoning.

- Exam-oriented formatting and mark-based answers.

- Coding problems or full program generation.

- Concept evaluation or correction  
  *(use Doubt Mode or Teach-Back Mode instead)*.

---

## 4. When to Use Fast Learn Mode

Use Fast Learn Mode:

- When you **do not have enough time** for detailed learning.

- During **last-minute revision** or quick recall.

- When you already know the topic and need a **rapid summary**.

---

## 5. When Not to Use Fast Learn Mode

Do **not** use Fast Learn Mode:

- When learning a topic for the first time.

- When you need **deep understanding or full explanations**.

- For **coding-related or implementation-based questions**.

In these cases, switch to **Learn Mode**.

---

## 6. Core Principle

> Fast Learn Mode prioritizes speed and recall over completeness.

It exists to support **quick understanding**, not comprehensive learning.
